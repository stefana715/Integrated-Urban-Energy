We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled **"Integrated assessment of building retrofit, rooftop photovoltaics, and climate resilience at city scale: an open-data framework applied to 18,826 buildings in Changsha, China"** for consideration for publication in *Applied Energy*.

**Why this paper is appropriate for Applied Energy**

*Applied Energy* regularly publishes city-scale building energy studies and urban decarbonisation analyses, and this manuscript directly advances three themes prominent in the journal's recent issues: (i) urban building energy modelling at scale, (ii) integrated retrofit-PV system assessment, and (iii) climate scenario impacts on building energy demand. Our study provides, to our knowledge, the first open-data framework to simultaneously quantify all three dimensions for a Chinese HSCW residential core at individual-building resolution.

**Key findings**

The combined R5 retrofit and rooftop PV strategy applied to Changsha's 18,826-building urban residential core reduces net energy demand by 44.3% and avoids 4,127 kt CO₂/yr — a carbon mitigation impact 4.10 times larger than photovoltaic deployment alone. We demonstrate that airtightness improvement (R4) is the single highest-leverage retrofit measure, delivering 27.8% of baseline demand in annual savings from pre-code residential stock. Our integrated four-dimensional grid priority scoring framework identifies 22 priority intervention grids (44% of the top 50) that are invisible to solar-only screening, and we show that delayed deployment forfeits 51 Mt CO₂ in cumulative savings by 2080.

**Novelty and significance**

The framework is built entirely on open data (OpenStreetMap, JRC Global Human Settlement Layer, pvlib) and is designed for direct transferability to other HSCW cities. The full codebase and processed data are released at [GitHub repository URL] under an open licence.
